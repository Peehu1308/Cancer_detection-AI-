What is Cancer?

Cancer is a generic term for a large group of diseases that can affect any part of the body. The defining feature of cancer is the rapid creation of abnormal cells that grow beyond their usual boundaries and invade adjoining parts of the body, spreading to other organs. This process is referred to as metastasis, the primary cause of death from cancer.

Common Cancers in 2020:
Breast (2.26 million cases);
Lung (2.21 million cases);
Colon and rectum (1.93 million cases);
Prostate (1.41 million cases);
Skin (non-melanoma) (1.20 million cases);
Stomach (1.09 million cases).
